Draw the Line

Math can tell you the time.

It can tell you how it’s ticking,

Unchanging, valuable, yet flipping

Through the contours of your life.

The relationship between y and x

Has never been more compatible,

No matter what you try,

You can never split these variables.

Feelings that we put aside,

Are impossible to define.

‘Cause values mean what they are,

‘But not the “I love you” I said from afar.

But it’s complicated to flick

One’s own ego and drive.

And it’s complicated to stick

To your own rules and thrive.

Why is it so hard?

To use my mind instead of my heart?

I follow no pattern, no trend, no line.

I’m more inconsistent than a cosine.

And there goes my family,

Crossing the heavenly boundary.

Years of testing and predicting,

To prove that my time was fleeting.

For all that weakness I lost myself,

What’s perfection without someone’s help?

Because of me, I didn’t say goodbye,

So, when will I draw the line?

Math can tell you the time.

It can tell you that it’s ticking.

Unchanging. Valuable. Limiting.

So you better draw the line.
